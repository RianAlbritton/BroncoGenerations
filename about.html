About

Ford came out with the Bronco in 1966. It was marketed as the original “All Purpose Vehicle” and was titled as a Station Wagon as the term Sport Utility Vehicle had not been coined yet. From 1966-1977, the Bronco had the same body style although there were several package options one could choose. Each package had a uniqueness to suit the desires of the buyers.

Sport Package

  • Chrome trim around windows
  • Bright red Ford lettering on the grille
  • Chrome taillight and marker light bezels
  • Exclusive SPORT emblem on the fender
  • Chrome horn ring
  • Pleated door panels

Ranger Package

  • White striping on the body and the hood
  • Bucking horse logo on the spare tire cover
  • Houndstooth vinyl upholstery
  • Full color-keyed carpeting
  • Color-keyed trim panels with wood-tone accents

Explorer Package

  • Introduced in 1972
  • Budget-conscious alternative
  • Select features form other two packages
  • EXPLORER badge on the glove box
  • EXPLORER spare tire cover
